Jonah Riley Huggins

Research

Jonah's work has primarily been in the creation of an adapted western blotting technique, known as 'The Mesowestern Blot', capable of characterizing upwards of 272 cellular lysates. Jonah joined the project in 2018 as an undergraduate Creative Inquiry student and has spearheaded the design and direction of the project since 2020. Here he developed a strong interest in CAD design and prototyping and from this looks to integrate such experience into future projects wherever applicable. Jonah designed and prototyped two SDS-PAGE Casting & Electrophoresis systems for implementing mesowestern blotting. Currently, the Mesowestern team been successful in creating methodology and devices that boast higher quality protein data from 10 times more samples, with 10 times less material required per sample and reduced labor compared to conventional methods. The Mesowestern technique achieves this using 3D parametric modeling, SDS-PAGE electrophoresis, and robotic pipetting, excelling in simplicity.

Jonah is interested in biomolecular research & computational biology aiding in proteomics investigation and building healthcare infrastructure. Contributing to proteomics to help map global protein dynamics and creating rapid biomolecular screenings is one of his greatest desires. Jonah aims to create multifaceted projects that break out of academia, spinning real world applications, both domestic and abroad.
