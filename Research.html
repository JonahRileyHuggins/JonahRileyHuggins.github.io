Jonah Riley Huggins

Research

Jonah's work is split between Whole-Cell Modeling efforts in the Birtwistle lab at Clemson University, and innovation in western blotting practice. Whole Cell Models aim to simulate the behavior of a cell in silico at the gene and gene-product level. Here, Jonah works on the SPARCED model, a large-scale mechanistic model of 6 hallmark cancer pathways. His work focuses on expanding the scope of the model through pathway integration.
Collaborating with the Birtwistle lab and Blotting Innovations, LLC, he has also developed a modified version of the western blot utilizing the familiar footprint of 96-well plates, enabling high-thoughput analysis. Jonah joined the project in 2018 as an undergraduate Creative Inquiry student and has spearheaded the design and direction of the project since 2020.

Jonah is interested in biomodeling as it pertains to cancer and aging research. He aims to create multifaceted projects that break out of academia, spinning real world applications, both domestic and abroad.
