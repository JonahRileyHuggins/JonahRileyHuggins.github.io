Jonah Riley Huggins

In The Lab

Jonah is a multifaceted scientist with a diverse experience in life science research. He's worked in 7 different labs over the course of 5 years, each one bringing a unique perspective. With each role he added another technique to his "scientific tool-belt" of skills within a wet lab environment. At 18 he began working part time in Dr. Jeremy Tzeng's laboratory as a lab assistant (2017). While the job was small, autoclaving, dish-washing, & media formulations, it was all the exposure he needed to understand he loved the environment. After spending a year as a lab assistant for Dr. Tzeng he undertook an externship within the Bioninformatics department of UNC Charlotte (2018). Under Dr. Cynthia Gibas he participated in weekly discussions about The Urban Environmental Genome Project. During this time graduate students under her supervision would demonstrate basic techniques of metagenomcis. This was both his first experience coding and with computational biology. In the following Fall semester (2018) he stepped into Dr. Marc Birtwistle's lab within the Chemical Engineering Dept. of Clemson University. From this initiating role he's gained an irreplacible amount of knowledge. From creative inquiry student researcher, lab assistant, research assistant, and even co-founder of Blotting Innovations LLC (2021), these roles have provided a plethora of experience.

Impromptu-Techie

Jonah's dawn and twilight hours occupy themselves chasing eclectic tech builds and programming projects. As a traditional life-science lab rat he'd never thought that being a "techie" would be in the cards, but who chooses what life has in store for them? Academic and Industry experience goes hand- in-hand for Jonah, but when Jonah thinks of "fun" he either opts for exercise or designing, building, losing control of, then conquering a weird machination. Either functional or impractical, Jonah will surmount the project with at least one positive note; life's a learning experience.
Jonah thoroughly enjoys taking on new programming languages, actively seeking out projects allowing him to do so. For instance, this website is his first exposure to all of front end development (that might be obvious). Whether its tinkering with a new raspberry pi based project, using FreeCAD 3D design as an excuse to write in python, or adapting ASTM methods into an R pipeline for data interpretation, Jonah is just doing it for fun
